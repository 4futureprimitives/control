Waves

A contingency of oceans, a pulse of sonic ruptures in the "new waves" that mark time. In the modern sense, the wave is the ultimate contradiction to control. Henri Lefebreve pointed to waves as the uncertain momentum of modernity, a legacy that attends the oppositional movements of democratization as much as the push against convention in art, music and film. From an indigenous perspective, the wave is also an epistemological beginning in a future-oriented push. From the social sciences to computer music, the wave is a contant movement of numbers that gives rise to an authoritarian desire for projection, representation, and replication.
